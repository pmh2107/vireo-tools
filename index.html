Job Description Evaluator

Built with ADHD in mind, useful for anyone whose brain doesn't love chaos.

Certain phrases tend to appear frequently in job descriptions for roles that ultimately become difficult to sustain for non-neurotypical brains. None of these phrases are inherently bad, but when they appear in clusters, they're worth pausing on. This filter flags language that may signal high cognitive load, unclear expectations, or environments where masking becomes a full-time job.

These aren't dealbreakers necessarily; they're data points to bear in mind.

Smoke Signals: Spot Potential Burnout Before You Apply describes the criteria in detail, along with clarifying questions to help you extract signal from noise.

Red flag patterns

  • "Fast-paced environment" — frequent context-switching, unpredictable priorities, pile-ons
  • "Wears many hats" — unclear role lanes, ill-defined responsibilities, understaffed teams
  • Resilience / thick skin language — normalized pressure, high conflict, poor planning culture
  • Vague responsibilities — unclear ownership, undefined success metrics
  • "High visibility" — always-on culture, meeting overload, little protected focus time
  • Heavy communication skills emphasis — informal politics, unspoken social dynamics

Green signals

  • Clear expectations — defined scope, explicit success metrics, structured onboarding
  • Autonomy language — flexibility, trust, ownership of your work
  • Psychological safety signals — mentorship culture, async communication, remote/flexible options
